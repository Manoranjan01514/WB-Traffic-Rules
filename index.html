West Bengal Motor Vehicle Guide

Welcome!

This website provides awareness about road rules, traffic fines, and complaint procedures in West Bengal under the Indian Motor Vehicles Act.

Information is referenced from the Transport Department, Government of West Bengal.

Rules & Fines for Drivers (West Bengal)

  • Section 129: Helmet mandatory for two-wheeler riders — ₹1000
  • Section 177: General violation — ₹500
  • Section 183: Over-speeding — ₹1000 (LMV), ₹2000 (HMV)
  • Section 185: Drunk driving — ₹10,000 or 6 months jail
  • Section 194B: Not wearing seat belt — ₹1000
  • Section 194D: Using mobile while driving — ₹5000
  • Section 128 + 177: Triple riding on two-wheeler — ₹1000
  • Section 190(2): No valid Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate — ₹10,000
  • Section 192: Driving with tampered/illegal number plate — ₹5000
  • Section 184: Dangerous driving / jumping signal — ₹5000
  • Section 194: Overloading of goods or passengers — ₹2000 + ₹1000 per extra person
  • WB Local Rule: No honking in silence zones — ₹1000
  • Fines are payable via eChallan Portal

Traffic Police Responsibilities (West Bengal)

Traffic Police must follow proper procedures defined under the Motor Vehicles Act:

  • Section 130: Driver must produce license, RC, insurance etc. when asked by an authorized officer.
  • Section 132: Police can signal a vehicle to stop.
  • Section 206: Police can seize the license in certain cases (dangerous driving, drunk driving etc.).
  • Section 210B: Penalty on enforcing authority for violation of provisions.
  • Who can check license or documents?
    • Only officers **not below the rank of Sub-Inspector (SI)** are legally allowed to check your documents.
    • Home Guard or Civic Volunteers cannot issue challans or force you to show documents.
    • They can assist but **not enforce** under MVA.
  • Who can issue challan?
    • Only officers of the rank of **Sub-Inspector (SI)** and above can issue challans.
    • They must issue an official receipt or eChallan using handheld devices or official portal.
  • Important: Always ask for name, ID, and badge number of the officer.
  • Visit official site: West Bengal Traffic Police

Complain Against Traffic Police (West Bengal)

If any traffic officer misbehaves, demands bribe, or violates protocol, you can lodge a complaint:

How to Complain:

  1. Note officer's name, badge number, date, time, and location.
  2. Capture video/audio proof (if safe).
  3. Complain through:
    • PG Portal: https://pgportal.gov.in
    • Visit: Nearest police station or traffic guard office.
    • Call: 103 – West Bengal Traffic Helpline
